Evulation of Dr.Martens

How did Dr.Martens get to where they are today

1901

Beginning of Everything

The Griggs family were known for making boots in the small town of Wollaston, Northamptonshire in the English Midlands. Griggs’ footwear earned a solid reputation as, durable work boots.

1961

Dr. Martens’ First Few Years of Existence Was Very Much That of a £2 Work-Wear Boot

Dr. Martens’ first few years of existence was very much that of a £2 work-wear boot, selling solid quantities to Britain’s working classes. Then something incredible started to happen..

1970

By the End of the Decade the Boot Had Become a Symbol of Self Expression

The decade of glam, punk, Two Tone and goth saw British youth culture mushroom into distinct tribes. Each new release the boot subverted the style of the previous wearers championed Dr. Martens.

1980

Trend for Girls Buying Then Customizing the Simple 8 Eye Boot

With Britain plagued by anti-government riots and social resentment, youth culture rose up from the streets with yet more highly visual and individual tribes such as psychobilly, grebo and scooter boys.

1990

The Brand Became Synonymous With Festival Culture

Grunge turned the mainstream music world on its head and took Dr. Martens along for the ride. Back in Britain, Britpop rebelled against this so-called ‘loser kid’ apathy but did so in the same boots.

2000s

Five Decades That Have Witnessed the Brands Adoption by Diverse Individuals

In the era of the global village and social media, every aspect of youth culture and subcultural style has changed. Yet diverse individuals, fans and subcultures still champion Dr. Martens.
